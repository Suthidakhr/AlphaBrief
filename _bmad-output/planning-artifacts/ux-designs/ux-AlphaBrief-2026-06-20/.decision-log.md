## Decisions Made — 2026-06-20

### D-001: Color Balance → Option C "Rich Blend"
- Nav bg: espresso (#4A342A)
- Daily Brief card header bg: espresso (#4A342A) — deliberate premium anchor
- Page bg: deep linen (#EDE8E0)
- Card bg: white (#FFFFFF)
- Card borders: khaki (#D7C9B8) — visible warmth, replaces hairline gray
- Section dividers: khaki (#D7C9B8)
- Rationale: Most layered and premium feel. Solves "too heavy" by confining espresso fills to nav + brief header only, while khaki borders give structure without adding espresso fills to content.

### D-002: News Card Hierarchy → Option B (Headline First)
- Scan order: headline (bold, largest) + sentiment badge (top-right pill) → AI insight box (linen bg, camel left border, no label) → stock impact badges → footer row (source · timestamp · category tag)
- No label inside the AI insight box — content speaks for itself
- Sentiment badge: dot + text pill (e.g. ● BEARISH) — satisfies WCAG non-color requirement
- Click card → dedicated detail page with permalink (no inline expand)
- Featured cards retain camel left-border accent

---

### D-003: Trends Page → Editorial Cards
- Theme cards: name + full 2–3 sentence description + sentiment badge + article count
- **Most recent article headline shown as a preview on the card** (updated from earlier "no preview" decision)
- Preview is visually secondary — small italic or muted weight below the description, labeled "Latest:"
- Spacious layout — fewer cards per screen, more breathing room than News feed
- Click → dedicated theme detail page showing all constituent articles with individual AI analysis
- Themes ordered by most recent constituent article (descending)

### D-004: Mobile Layout → Stacking Order
- Bottom tab bar: Overview / News / Stocks / Trends (single tap to any section)
- Home page mobile stack (top to bottom): Daily Brief card → News feed → sidebar widgets (Market Overview, Sector Heatmap, Trend Summary) below the fold
- Sidebar widgets are accessible by scrolling — not hidden, not moved to separate tab

### D-007: Sentiment Color Visibility — Increase Vividness
- Previous tokens were too subtle for at-a-glance recognition (positive: #2d6a4f, negative: #c1121f)
- Updated to more vivid but still professional values:
  - positive text: #16a34a (green-600) — clearly readable bullish green
  - positive-bg: #dcfce7 (green-100) — visible wash, not pastel
  - positive-border: #86efac (green-300)
  - negative text: #dc2626 (red-600) — clearly readable bearish red
  - negative-bg: #fee2e2 (red-100) — visible wash
  - negative-border: #fca5a5 (red-300)
- Applies to: sentiment badges, sector heatmap cells, stock impact badges, market movement indicators
- Goal: immediate at-a-glance distinction WITHOUT creating urgency or trading-terminal energy

### D-008: Daily Brief Position — Desktop Sidebar (Confirmed)
- Desktop: Daily Brief stays in right sidebar — news feed is primary content, brief is supporting intelligence
- Mobile: Daily Brief moves to top of stack above news feed — becomes the primary entry point on small screens
- Rationale: on desktop users have space to see both simultaneously; on mobile, the brief orients users before they scroll into the feed

### D-009: Design Philosophy — Finalized
- Clarity · Trust · Context · Readability · Calm decision support
- Avoid: prediction language, trading signals, recommendation wording, urgency-driven design patterns
- "Research companion, not trading platform" — users feel confident to do their own research after using AlphaBrief; the final decision is always theirs

### D-005: Bilingual Identity → Permanent
- Bilingual nav labels (English primary, Thai subtitle) are a permanent identity element, not a placeholder
- Audience: Thai retail investors who understand English financial terms but prefer Thai for clarity
- Scope: nav labels and section headers only in MVP; full Thai-language UI is post-MVP

### D-006: Emotional Target → Informed, Calm, Prepared
- NOT urgent/alarming (not a trading floor) — warm, measured tone
- Error states and staleness indicators must reassure, not alarm
- "Morning coffee research session" — the product helps you understand before you act
